# Jerry’s Visual Style Guide

## Collection reviewed
45 images from **“45 may you know piece.zip.”**

## Core style
**Contemplative visionary magical realism** — cinematic, spiritually resonant images that place ordinary human experience beside something immense, luminous, impossible, or sacred. The work is emotionally warm and benevolent rather than ominous. It often uses a single clear visual metaphor to turn an abstract idea into a coherent scene.

## Style fingerprint

### Composition
- One dominant focal point, usually centred or strongly framed.
- Symmetry or near-symmetry is common.
- Windows, doorways, balconies, paths, bridges, rivers, aisles, and horizons create strong leading lines.
- Layered foreground, middle ground, and background produce depth.
- Tiny people, small groups, or a lone observer often establish monumental scale.
- The composition is detailed but orderly, with a readable central idea.

### Colour
- Deep indigo, midnight blue, teal, turquoise, and blue-grey.
- Warm amber, copper, ochre, ivory, cream, and soft gold.
- Cool celestial shadows balanced by warm human or spiritual light.
- Occasional bright colour is used as an accent rather than as an overall neon treatment.

### Light
- A strong luminous source: moon, stars, sunrise, fire, halo, portal, open book, or unseen radiance.
- Backlighting, rim light, soft halos, god rays, and volumetric atmosphere.
- Warm light often enters from one side while the opposite side remains cool and shadowed.
- Light functions symbolically, not merely decoratively.

### Rendering
- Highly detailed cinematic digital painting.
- Near-photorealistic forms with painterly softness and idealized polish.
- Tactile stone, wood, cloth, clouds, foliage, paper, and water.
- Atmospheric depth, soft mist, luminous particles, and controlled glow.
- More like a finished visionary illustration or digital oil painting than a literal photograph.

### Emotional tone
- Reverent, compassionate, contemplative, peaceful, mysterious, and quietly awe-filled.
- Hopeful and spiritually open without becoming preachy.
- Human figures are dignified and inward-looking rather than theatrical.
- The impossible is presented as natural and believable.

### Recurring imagery
- Wise elders, teachers, compassionate faces, blessing gestures.
- Children and adults together; inner-child imagery.
- Libraries, books, open tomes, roads or towers made of books.
- Ancient trees, mountains, meadows, rivers, clouds, moonlit rooms, monasteries.
- Windows and thresholds opening onto vast alternative worlds.
- Sacred geometry, celestial bodies, halos, constellations, luminous diagrams.
- Monumental scale: giant statues, cosmic skies, floating islands, impossible architecture.

## Reusable master prompt

Create a contemplative work of **visionary magical realism** that transforms **[IDEA OR EMOTION]** into one clear, coherent visual metaphor.

Place **[PRIMARY SUBJECT]** as the dominant focal point, centred or strongly framed within the composition. Use **[PATH / WINDOW / DOORWAY / RIVER / BRIDGE / HORIZON / SMALL HUMAN FIGURES]** to guide the eye toward it and establish a sense of journey, threshold, or monumental scale. Build distinct foreground, middle ground, and background layers. Blend a believable natural or human setting with one extraordinary, physically impossible element that feels spiritually meaningful rather than fantastical for its own sake.

Render the scene as a highly detailed, cinematic digital oil painting with near-photorealistic forms, tactile organic surfaces, painterly softness, and luminous atmospheric depth. Use a palette of deep indigo, midnight blue, teal, turquoise, blue-grey, warm amber, copper, ivory, cream, and soft gold. Balance cool celestial shadow with warm human or sacred illumination.

Use one meaningful source of radiance—moonlight, sunrise, firelight, a halo, an open book, a portal, stars, or concealed light—to create soft rim lighting, volumetric rays, gentle halos, mist, and layered clouds. The light should carry emotional and symbolic meaning.

The mood is compassionate, reverent, peaceful, mysterious, and quietly awe-filled—never threatening, cynical, kitschy, or melodramatic. People should appear natural, dignified, and contemplative. Use a lone observer, a small group, or tiny distant figures when useful for scale. Keep the image intricate but orderly, balanced, readable, and emotionally sincere.

No text unless specifically requested. No logos, watermarks, horror, dystopian menace, grotesque anatomy, exaggerated facial expressions, cheap plastic CGI, flat cartoon styling, generic stock-photo staging, or random decorative symbolism. Include sacred geometry or celestial imagery only when it supports the central idea.

**Format:** [ASPECT RATIO / DIMENSIONS].

## Short everyday style block

Visionary contemplative magical realism; one clear symbolic idea; cinematic digital oil painting with near-photorealistic forms and painterly softness; deep indigo, teal, turquoise, amber, copper, ivory, and soft gold; luminous atmospheric depth, volumetric light, mist, layered clouds, tactile organic detail; centred or strongly framed composition with paths, thresholds, horizons, or tiny figures creating scale; reverent, compassionate, peaceful, mysterious, and quietly awe-filled; intricate but orderly; no text, horror, dystopia, cheap CGI, cartoon styling, or visual clutter.

## Adjustable modules

### Landscape / monumental scene
Use a wide cinematic landscape with a dominant central landmark or impossible natural formation. Include a path, river, bridge, or tiny distant figures to convey journey and immense scale. Keep the edges darker and more organic, opening toward a luminous centre or horizon.

### Spiritual portrait
Create a close or medium portrait of a wise, compassionate person with natural age, expressive eyes, calm dignity, and a subtle benevolent expression. Use simple textured clothing, warm side light, a soft halo-like atmosphere rather than a literal religious icon, and an understated background of nature, books, stars, or architecture. Hands may be open, resting over the heart, or offering a gentle blessing, but should remain natural and anatomically correct.

### Spiritual gathering
Show a small, diverse group in quiet communion rather than formal worship. Arrange them in a circle, around a table, beneath a night sky, beside a fire, or within a warm interior. Emphasize shared attention, listening, and stillness. Keep all traditions visually respectful and avoid rigid denominational symbolism unless requested.

### Surreal conceptual metaphor
Translate **[ABSTRACT CONCEPT]** into one visually legible impossible scene. Combine ordinary reality with a single dominant transformation—for example, a road made of books, thoughts becoming a cosmos, a window opening onto another world, or machinery suspended over a meadow. The metaphor should be understood at a glance and supported by secondary details, not buried beneath them.

### Interior / threshold scene
Begin in a believable, intimate interior—bedroom, study, basement, library, cabin, or office—and open one wall, window, staircase, book, or doorway into a luminous impossible realm. Contrast warm human-scale details inside with cool cosmic or natural vastness beyond. Preserve a feeling of safety, wonder, and invitation.

### Cosmic / celestial scene
Use a deep indigo and teal sky filled with layered nebulae, stars, planets, pearl-like celestial bodies, delicate orbital curves, and subtle sacred geometry. Keep one grounded human observer, shoreline, balcony, mountain, or cloud layer to prevent the image from becoming decorative space art. The cosmos should feel musical, intelligent, and spiritually alive rather than technological.

### Text-overlay composition
Reserve 25–35% of the image as calm negative space with low detail and even tonal contrast. Place the main subject away from that area. Avoid bright stars, faces, branches, or high-contrast textures behind the intended words. Generate the artwork without text first; add exact wording afterward for reliable typography.

## Negative-constraint block

Avoid: ominous horror, apocalyptic destruction, bleak dystopia, aggressive action, grotesque or distorted anatomy, extra fingers, waxy faces, exaggerated smiles, theatrical posing, crowded visual noise, arbitrary symbols, garish neon, muddy brown colour casts, hard plastic 3D rendering, flat illustration, childish fantasy, generic fantasy armour, stock-photo realism, excessive lens flare, illegible text, logos, and watermarks.

## Consistency checklist

Before generating, specify:

1. The single abstract idea or emotional truth.
2. The one dominant visual metaphor.
3. The focal subject and its placement.
4. The scale cue: observer, group, path, window, bridge, or horizon.
5. The warm/cool palette balance.
6. The symbolic light source.
7. The desired degree of realism versus painterly softness.
8. The emotional tone: reverent, compassionate, peaceful, mysterious.
9. The aspect ratio and intended use.
10. Any elements that must remain absent.

## Translating older DALL·E prompting habits

Older DALL·E prompts often worked as long strings of visual ingredients. For current image generation, keep the descriptive vocabulary but add relationships and priorities:

- Do not merely list objects; explain which object dominates and how the others support it.
- Describe the visual metaphor before describing the decoration.
- State where the viewer stands and how the eye moves through the image.
- Define the emotional role of the light.
- Use one extraordinary element rather than several unrelated surreal elements.
- During revisions, say exactly what must remain unchanged before describing the requested change.
- When using a reference, specify whether it controls composition, style, palette, subject identity, or only mood.

## Recommended prompt order

1. Central idea and metaphor.
2. Main subject.
3. Composition and scale.
4. Environment and secondary details.
5. Light and palette.
6. Rendering style.
7. Emotional tone.
8. Exclusions.
9. Dimensions or aspect ratio.
